  1. What is an Elephant?
    2. a heavy plant-eating mammal with a prehensile trunk, long curved ivory tusks, and large ears, native to Africa and southern Asia.
    It is the largest living land animal.

  2. What is a lion?
    3. a large tawny-colored cat that lives in prides, found in Africa and northwestern India.
    The male has a flowing shaggy mane and takes little part in hunting, which is done cooperatively by the females.

  3. What is an eagle?
    4. a large bird of prey with a massive hooked bill and long broad wings, renowned for its keen sight and powerful soaring flight.

  4. What is a platypus?
    5. a semiaquatic egg-laying mammal which frequents lakes and streams in eastern Australia.
    It has a sensitive pliable bill shaped like that of a duck, webbed feet with venomous spurs, and dense fur.

  5. What is a tiger?
    6. a very large solitary cat with a yellow-brown coat striped with black, native to the forests of Asia but becoming increasingly rare.

  6. What is an ostrich?
    7. a flightless swift-running African bird with a long neck, long legs, and two toes on each foot.
    It is the largest living bird, with males reaching an average height of 8 feet (2.5 m).

  7. What is a toad?
    8. a tailless amphibian with a short stout body and short legs, typically having dry warty skin that can exude poison.
  8. What is a wolf?
    9. a wild carnivorous mammal of the dog family, living and hunting in packs.
    It is native to both Eurasia and North America, but has been widely exterminated.
  9. What is an emu?
    10. a large flightless fast-running Australian bird resembling the ostrich, with shaggy gray or brown plumage, bare blue skin on the head and neck, and three-toed feet.
  10. What is a flamingo?
    11. a tall wading bird with mainly pink or scarlet plumage and long legs and neck.
    It has a heavy bent bill that is held upside down in the water in order to filter-feed on small organisms.
